Ctrl-Z's Animal Allies Problem


Every year, 1 million pets are lost in Canada and only 22% are reunited with their families. This causes significant grief to both pets and their owners.


Existing Solutions For Finding Lost Pets


Many existing solution use cell phone towers to locate the pet. This requires a monthly subscription which can be very expensive. Other solutions notify nearby people that the animal is lost but require them to have an app on their mobile device. Not everyone would have this app and would, therefore, limit the effectiveness of the solution. Pip is a small GPS and activity tracker for dogs and cats.


Ctrl-Z Lost Pet Survey



The questions asked were:

  • Would you approach a dog or cat that appears to be lost?
  • Do you have a cat or a dog?
  • Why do you not have a dog or a cat?
  • Is your dog microchipped?
  • How does your dog react to normal human conversation happening around it?
  • Have you ever lost your dog?
  • Is your cat microchipped?
  • How does your cat react to normal human conversation happening around it?
  • Have you ever lost your cat?
  • Do you have a device for your pet to help find it in case it gets lost? (not including microchip)
  • Why do you not have a device to help find your lost pet?
  • Would you buy a device to help find your lost pet if it cost less than 30$?
  • Were you reunited with your pet?
  • Did you have a device to help find your lost pet? (not including microchip)

Conclusion:

This suggests that there is a high need for the Pinder Pet Finder as an inexpensive way to increase the chances of reuniting a lost pet with it's owner.
