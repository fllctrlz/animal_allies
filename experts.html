Claudia Vecchio		 Claudia Vecchio was born and raised in Milan, Italy. After completing her postgraduate degree in Political Science, majoring in International Law, at the University of Milan, she moved to Brussels where she undertook further postgraduate courses on Law of the European Union. After a short period of teaching at York University, she went into Real Estate.
Since her childhood, Claudia has shown a great love for animals and a particular attraction to cats. She has rescued stray and abandoned cats on her own for 25 years. In 2002, she realized that she could no longer carry out these massive rescue efforts by herself. She decided, with Corrine Thaw, to found ORA - Organization for the Rescue of Animals - a rescue organization that obtained charitable status in November 2002.
Claudia's dream is to create wildlife sanctuaries throughout Southern Ontario - oasis where wildlife, endangered by urban sprawl, may find quiet and peaceful areas in which to live undisturbed.

Casey Panning		 Casey Panning is the Community Outreach & Education Coordinator at the Toronto Humane Society. Her role at the Humane Society is to teach young and old alike about animal welfare, responsible pet ownership, and compassion for living things. This is achieved through field trips to the shelter in addition to in-class workshops. Casey completed her Bachelor of Education in the Intermediate/Senior division at OISE (University of Toronto) in 2012, and recently completed a Master's in Public Health at the Ontario Veterinary College (University of Guelph) in 2016. Her current role integrates her two passions of teaching and animal welfare.

Jack Feder		 Jack Feder is an Engineering Science graduate from the University of Toronto and now works as an electrical engineer designing innovative electronic medical instruments.

Cathy Broughton		 Cathy Broughton, founder of Cathy Broughton Dog Walking, is a professional dog walker in our community.

Galen Udell		 Galen Udell is a business and marketing graduate from Simon Fraser University. He has worked in multiple companies including IBM and a cable and infrastructure company. He is now pursuing his lifelong passion by starting Bark ‘n Yapp, a website to create an online community for dog lovers and related businesses.

Lucas Chang		 Lucas Chang is working with companies to identify what was holding them back from achieving profitability or growth targets, and helping them plan and overcome those plateaus. Along the way, he also became the community lead for Startup York Region, (a chapter of Startup Canada), which brings together entrepreneurs from across the region. Working with partners in the ecosystem to foster an environment that encouraged more startups, he co-created the EIR (Entrepreneurs in Residence) speaker series, where entrepreneurs share their journeys and stories in TEDx-like fashion, ran a Startup Weekend, and co-founded the Y2 Entrepreneurship Labs, dedicated to helping teenagers learn about entrepreneurship and innovation by connecting them with entrepreneur mentors. Since January 2016, he has also been managing PerfectlySoft’s daily operations, and working with developers from Toronto and across the globe to help them learn how to use the server-side Swift framework Perfect. Recognized as a shift disturber in York Region, he has been invited to speak about startups and startup ecosystems at various schools and universities, on the radio, and on podcast.
